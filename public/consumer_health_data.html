Consumer Health Data Privacy Notice

Last Updated: July 21, 2025

Legal Disclaimer: This document is a template and does not constitute legal advice. You must consult with a qualified legal professional to ensure your policies are fully compliant with all applicable laws, including but not limited to Washington's My Health My Data Act and other state-level privacy regulations.

This notice supplements our main Privacy Policy and provides specific disclosures required by certain state laws regarding the collection, use, and sharing of "Consumer Health Data."

1. Categories of Consumer Health Data We Collect

To provide the ThriveOn service, we may collect the following categories of data that may be considered Consumer Health Data:

  • Health Conditions and Diagnoses: Information about specific health conditions, treatments, symptoms, and diseases related to you or the person you are caring for.
  • Medication and Treatment Information: Data related to prescriptions, dosages, schedules, and other treatments managed by the Logistics Agent.
  • Healthcare Appointments and Services: Information about past and future appointments with healthcare providers, including specialists and therapists.
  • Measurements and Vital Signs: Any bodily function or vital sign data you choose to track within the Platform.
  • Mental and Emotional Health Information: Data related to your emotional state, stress levels, feelings of burnout, and other mental health indicators, often inferred from your interactions with the Connection Agent.
  • Data Identifying You as Seeking Health Care Services: Your use of the ThriveOn platform itself may be considered an indicator that you are seeking health care services or information for yourself or another person.

2. Sources of Consumer Health Data

We collect the Consumer Health Data listed above directly from you when you voluntarily provide it through your use of the ThriveOn Platform.

3. Purpose of Collection and Use (Why We Collect It)

We collect and use Consumer Health Data for one primary purpose: to operate, maintain, and provide the features of the ThriveOn Platform to you. This includes:

  • Powering the AI agents (MyThri, Logistics Agent, Connection Agent) so they can provide you with relevant support, reminders, and prompts.
  • Allowing you to track and manage caregiving information effectively.
  • Personalizing your experience to make the service as helpful as possible.

4. Categories of Third Parties With Whom We Share Data

We do not sell your Consumer Health Data. We only share it with the following categories of third parties who are essential to providing our service:

  • Service Providers: We share data with companies that provide technical infrastructure for our Platform, such as cloud hosting services (e.g., Amazon Web Services). These providers are contractually bound to protect your data and cannot use it for their own purposes.

We do not share your data with data brokers or any other third parties for marketing or advertising purposes.

5. How to Exercise Your Rights

You have the right to access, delete, or withdraw your consent for the collection and use of your Consumer Health Data. You can exercise these rights by:

  • Using the data management tools within your account settings (if applicable).
  • Contacting us directly at the email address below to submit a request.

To appeal a decision we have made regarding your request, please contact us at privacy@thriveon.app.
